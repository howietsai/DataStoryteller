Historical IPOs and Profitability

With all the talk of winter and souring investor sentiment in Silicon Valley, it is interesting to look back at the trends of IPOs and their possible roles at indicating troubled markets. The years 2000 and 2007 correspond to some of the lowest percentages of profitable IPOs in the tech industry, as well as the last two major public market corrections, the dotcom bubble of 2000 and the recent financial disaster of 2008. What's troubling is that while we have not reached the sheer volume of IPOs of the dotcom boom, we have percentages of profitable IPOs in 2014 that are lower than was faced in 2000.

Other Industry IPOs and Profitability

The tech industry has always been treated and seen differently from investors, who invest more in growth opportunity rather than pure business metrics. As an industry, Technology and Biotechnology Companies are more than twice as likely to IPO while still unprofitable.
